Reimagining the history of technology

In the 1980s, "No Bad Memories" was the slogan of an American floppy-disk manufacturer. Appropriated for my own purposes, "No Bad Memories" serves as a reflection of the relationship between computing history and nostalgia. If nostalgia for classic video games and computers is born of an idealized, imagined version of the past, why are its aesthetics so congruent? And what viewpoints does that congruence privelege? My work reimagines obsolete technology against the grain of mainstream, commercial nostalgia to dream of alternative futures.
